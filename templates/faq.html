Frequently Asked Questions

Here is everything you ever wanted to know about TrustRoots. If you can’t find your answer here, message us at info@trustrootsnetwork.org

What is the problem you are looking to solve?

Despite a global aid budget of over 140 billion USD annually, much of this money never actually reaches local organisations, let alone the communities they serve. 95% of US and 90% of UK´s Official Development Assistance (ODA) contracts are awarded within the donor countries themselves. Only 1.2-3%of global humanitarian aid go to local or national organisations. Meanwhile intermediaries take significant overheads, often calculated at 15% but going upwards of 40%, to implement themselves from Global Minority countries (often referred to as the Global North), building large international, bureaucratic structures to do so, or even just for redistribution to smaller, local organisations. Local grassroots are often not trusted to handle international aid funds effectively beyond immediate programmatic costs. As a result, the actors doing the most important, immediate work with affected communities often lack the core operating funding they need to work effectively, efficiently and sustainably. This ultimately results in “so-called” beneficiaries not receiving the support they need. In the global aid & philanthropy sector we thus seek to tackle: Financial Inequity: Financial imbalances between donors and grassroots. Meaning only 1.2% of global humanitarian aid goes directly to local organisations Low Effectiveness: Limited access to core and flexible funding prevents grassroots from sustainable implementation in their community’s best interest Power Imbalance: Prioritisation and decision making power are still too often held by donors, rather than local actors and their communities

How do you define grassroots?

TrustRoots defines grassroots, as small to medium sized movements, groups or organisations, directly embedded with the communities they serve. Whilst we don’t limit the number of employees, we believe the following criteria are important: Community-based: Grassroots organisations are rooted in local communities and are formed by individuals who are directly affected by a particular issue or cause. The organisation's strength and support come from the members of a community who share common concerns and goals. Bottom-up approach: Decision-making and leadership in the organisation is inclusive and emerges from the grassroots level rather than being imposed from a central authority. Focus on local issues: Grassroots organisations address issues that are relevant to the community or specific groups of people. Independence: Grassroots organisations operate independently of larger institutions or governmental bodies, allowing them to maintain a degree of autonomy and responsiveness to local needs

Do I have to pay to have my grassroots organisation featured on TrustRoots?

No, TrustRoots is completely free of charge for grassroots organisations.

Where does the TrustRoots network of grassroots come from?

We are an open network, available for grassroots organisations from across Global Majority Countries. Based on our existing networks and experience, our pilot is currently focused on India, Kenya and Tanzania. However we are very open to feature grassroots from additional countries, as we progress. The network initially grew from our existing networks, as well as the networks of our partner organisations Athari Group and Amani Institute. Beyond that, we work with independent ambassadors who are based in the respective countries and reach out to relevant local grassroots actors.

How does your vetting process work?

TrustRoots vetting is ensured through a network of independent ambassadors and expert reviewers. Ambassadors from the respective countries are the first point of contact, verifying the existence and reputation of the organisation. To set up a profile on TrustRoots grassroots are required to provide baseline documents and links, which are then reviewed by experts from the development sector. We are not aiming to be exclusive or comprehensive in our presentation of organisations, but rather use the review as an opportunity to support organisations to improve their profile and visibility. Instead, our focus in vetting and due diligence lies in the reviews and vetting of the community members themselves. Just like with booking.com or airbnb - what we believe counts most are the voices by people who the grassroots seeks to service. Beyond that, each donor or funder is expected to review the organisations in full based on their own due diligence processes.

What are the criteria for joining TrustRoots?

To be represented on TrustRoots your organisation should be community-based, have a bottom-up approach, focus on local issues, and be independent. In the registration process you will be asked to provide the following information: Number of community members you support Your mission, vision, values and types of activities Proof of at least two activities Examples of how you identify your communities needs. Minimum one community member review Contact details Registration certificates are requested but not required. We recommend you provide proof/reasoning for why you are not registered should these not be available

What is your theory of change?

At TrustRoots we believe that if we create a trust-focused partnership platform for grassroots visibility and peer-review, then donors are more likely to trust the organisations. If there is more trust in the partnerships, small grassroots across Global Majority countries will have easier access to flexible funding opportunities. If grassroots have access to flexible funding opportunities then they will deliver effective, impactful & sustainable programs and have the ability to build strong & resilient organisations. We believe that our unique, data-driven, peer-review model, focused on lived-experiences of the impact organisations will facilitate trust based partnerships. In addition we will of course look to facilitate this mindset through partnering with and supporting existing advocacy efforts, as well as providing peer-learning and collaboration opportunities amongst grassroots. The platform will help donors move beyond tools such as feedback surveys or site visits and lean into the work of discovering more effective ways to deeply listen to the needs of the communities they work with.

What is your definition of trust?

The Oxford dictionary defines trust as: “the firm belief in the reliability, truth, or ability of someone or something - as well as the acceptance of the truth of a statement without evidence or investigation”. This resonates with our vision and mission, and at the same time shows: Trust is vital in both the grassroots-donor, but also grassroots-community relationships, to ensure the work can be effective. We believe building trust takes time. Intentionally creating time for difficult conversations about power, privilege, and equity on a regular basis and being open to new possibilities and transformation, even when they are difficult or have potential risks, are necessary to create a trust-oriented culture. TrustRoots exists to challenge existing power inequities between donors and grassroots, and foster more trust-based partnerships that centre on the feedback and trust of communities, rather than formalised due diligence processes. This will allow aid to be based on specific needs of culture and place, and help inform opportunities to connect more deeply with communities and thus make sure their work is more sustainable and effective - as well as inherently equitable.

How does TrustRoots sustain itself?

We are currently largely volunteer led, working with our own finances and funding from the Athari Group Accelerator for our beta version. To fully build out our digital product, optimise our outreach strategy, provide significant value to grassroots and fairly compensate ambassadors in the focus countries - we are constantly on the look-out for additional funding and non-monetary support opportunities, as well as testing additional revenue opportunities. If you have an idea, please do reach out: info@trustrootsnetwork.org

How can I support TrustRoots?

We are always looking for support to build and maintain TrustRoots. If you do not have the necessary finances, your time and expertise would also be invaluable. We are looking for support in these three areas - go to the homepage to sign up: Ambassadors: Ambassadors representatives from each country that are responsible for grassroots outreach and onboarding in their region/country. Depending on their additional skills and background, as well as availability, they may also support translation, peer-learning/advocacy session support and/or marketing. For this we are looking for people who have in-depth knowledge of and a network within local grassroots organisations, are value-driven individuals with a thorough understanding of or interest in acquiring knowledge in grassroots needs and local compliance requirements. In return we seek to offer certified and accompanied work experience for an international organisation, travel and data reimbursement where possible, the possibility to connect with and learn from inspiring grassroots founders, and access to international donor networks. Expert reviewers Expert reviewers are qualified professionals from the development space, who are seeking to contribute to lasting change in the sector. Ideally, they would have partnership, due diligence and/or evaluation experience - with a power-sensitive mindset and willingness to learn from ambassadors and grassroots alike. There is a strong preference for reviewers from Global Majority countries - with engagement time fully flexible. In return, we look to offer an opportunity to contribute to a power shift within the sector, gain experience understanding the needs and contributions of various grassroots organisations across the globe, as well as a certified volunteering certificate.

My country isn’t represented on TrustRoots - how can it get started there?

We started in India, Tanzania and Kenya because we have some combined experience in those places. However, if you have a network or interest in a certain country and would like to see grassroots from there represented on TrustRoots - we would love to hear from you. Just sign up via the ambassador outreach form and let us know where you would like to get started. We will provide you with all the outreach and communication content you need and look forward to getting to know grassroots from across the world together with you.

How do you seek to guarantee reliable reviews from communities?

Our ambassadors and early-onboarded grassroots will be trained on how best to collect reviews from communities. These can be collected in any format and should focus on stories and the lived experiences of communities, rather than externally defined indicators. We will also provide additional materials/guidance and, and where possible public outreach campaigns to reach communities beyond the links provided through grassroots themselves. We will also be looking to record explanatory, multilingual videos for donors, grassroots and communities - to outline the functionalities of the platform, the added value for them - as well as communities’ opportunities for contribution. Communities are encouraged to provide feedback through registering with their email, phone or social media accounts. However, we of course also want to allow for anonymous feedback to be allowed. All comments will go through a review process to prevent hate speech or mis- and disinformation being published, before going live. Grassroots in turn will have the opportunity to mark a comment as false, which will then go for review by the respective local ambassador and expert reviewer.

What do you mean by a data-driven approach?

The platform will help us create a repository of NGO profiles, donors, and other stakeholders. The data and insights gained into these grassroots, is important data to facilitate donors’ funding mechanisms, due diligence processes and understanding of grassroots strengths and needs. In addition, grassroots would find it valuable to know what type of grants are out there, as well as finding other organisations they could partner with. Combined, this data can be used to arrive at insights about the sectors and impact of funding local organisations directly, as well as much needed data on the extent to which aid money is actually being localised.

Is TrustRoots a registered entity?

We are currently in the process of registering as a legal entity in both India and Germany. In the meantime we have a fiscal sponsorship agreement with an existing registered partner in the USA who may act as a guarantor or intermediary where needed.
